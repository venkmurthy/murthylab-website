Not dark: the current theme with the white areas taking the band tone.

The accents had to change. The current amber #8F6410, cyan #3A7482 and violet #6B5B7B were darkened to clear WCAG AA against white. On a dark ground they score 3.45, 3.46 and 2.94 — all failing. Every variant here uses lightened values that clear 5:1 or better on its own ground.

What to look at. The publications page is the real test: 313 entries of small text is where a dark ground either reads comfortably or becomes tiring. These previews only show the home page, so if one appeals I would build it out across all pages before you decide.

A caution. Dark backgrounds suit interfaces better than they suit long-form reading. Your publications and research sections are substantial bodies of text, and light-on-dark is measurably slower to read for most people. Worth weighing against whatever is prompting the complaints.
